# WB Studio Brand Context

Portable brand reference for WB Studio, based on the current website at `wbstudio.xyz`. Use this when writing copy, planning content, designing social posts, or briefing another repo or agent.

## 1. Positioning

WB Studio is a boutique, founder-led architecture studio by Wael Bousmael for private villas and homes in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, and the Gulf.

The brand sits in the premium residential architecture space: minimal, calm, image-led, and editorial, with a strong focus on private villas, family living, modern Hijazi identity, coastal plots, and build-ready design clarity.

Core positioning in plain language:

- Arabic: `استوديو معماري بوتيكي لعملاء الفلل الخاصة في جدة والسعودية والخليج.`
- English: `A boutique architecture studio for private villa clients in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia and the Gulf.`

The studio should feel personal and expert, not corporate. It is a focused architectural practice with direct founder involvement, not a large production office.

## 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Private landowners in Jeddah, especially North Jeddah and Obhur.
- Saudi and GCC families planning to build a private villa.
- High-end residential clients who care about privacy, hospitality, long-term value, and architectural identity.
- Private investors or owners who want a refined villa, resort, majlis, or residential concept rather than a repeated template.

Audience mindset:

- They may own land and need help turning it into a clear architectural direction.
- They value privacy for the family and gracious hospitality for guests.
- They may be unsure about cost, process, required documents, or the difference between concept design and execution drawings.
- They want premium design, but not loud luxury.
- They need trust before paying: the website lowers risk through a free call and free preliminary concept.

Geographic focus:

- Primary: Jeddah.
- Strong sub-focus: Obhur and North Jeddah coastal plots.
- Broader service area: Saudi Arabia and GCC, in person or remotely.

## 3. Main Offer

The core offer must stay consistent:

1. Free call to review the plot.
2. Free preliminary concept.
3. Paid agreement only after the direction is clear and aligned.

Reusable offer copy:

- Arabic: `نبدأ بمكالمة مجانية وتصور مبدئي مجاني — ولا يبدأ أي اتفاق مدفوع إلا بعد وضوح الرؤية والتوافق.`
- English: `We begin with a free call and a free preliminary concept — a paid agreement starts only once the direction is clear and aligned.`

Process framing:

- Step 1: Review the plot, lifestyle goals, family needs, privacy, budget, and site conditions.
- Step 2: Discuss an initial architectural concept and interior atmosphere before commitment.
- Step 3: If aligned, move into a paid agreement and detailed design package.

The promise is clarity before commitment. Do not make the offer sound like a discount, giveaway, or generic consultation funnel.

## 4. Tone Of Voice

Arabic is the primary voice. It should be approachable, professional, calm, and confident. Avoid stiff classical Arabic and avoid slangy marketing language.

English should feel editorial and precise, like a premium architecture studio: calm, direct, tactile, and restrained.

Voice traits:

- Calm, transparent, and expert.
- Warm but not chatty.
- Premium but not flashy.
- Practical, especially around process, cost, documents, and buildability.
- Architectural and sensory: stone, light, proportion, shade, courtyards, timber, privacy, sea light.
- Honest about constraints: climate, privacy, budget, plot orientation, execution, coordination.

Preferred vocabulary:

- Arabic: `وضوح`, `توافق`, `خصوصية`, `ضيافة`, `نِسب مدروسة`, `مواد صادقة`, `ضوء طبيعي`, `تظليل`, `فناء`, `روشان`, `حجر`, `قيمة طويلة المدى`, `تصميم جاهز للتنفيذ`.
- English: `clarity`, `alignment`, `privacy`, `hospitality`, `considered proportions`, `honest materials`, `natural light`, `shade`, `courtyard`, `rawshan`, `stone`, `long-term value`, `build-ready design`.

Avoid hype. The brand should sound like an architect guiding a serious homeowner, not a real-estate salesperson.

## 5. Visual Identity

Overall feel:

- Minimal, premium, image-led, editorial.
- Warm, quiet, spacious, and architectural.
- Whitespace is a key design material.

Palette:

- Warm white: `#faf8f4`
- Charcoal: `#1e1c19`
- Stone: `#f2eee7`
- Warm grey: `#857f74`
- Hairline borders around 13% charcoal.

Typography:

- Arabic display: Amiri.
- Arabic body: IBM Plex Sans Arabic.
- English display: Cormorant Garamond.
- English body: Inter.
- Never letterspace Arabic.
- Uppercase and letterspacing are only appropriate in English.

Imagery:

- Editorial, muted, architectural.
- Prioritize real architecture, villas, stone, shadow, courtyards, terraces, pools, timber soffits, warm interiors, and coastal light.
- Covers should feel calm and premium, not like stock real-estate sales images.

Visual rules:

- No saturated colors.
- No gradients.
- No shadows.
- No decorative noise.
- No loud skies, watermarks, real-estate staging, or over-polished lifestyle cliches.
- Motion should be minimal: reveal fade and subtle image hover scale only.

## 10. Things To Avoid

Brand and copy:

- Do not make WB Studio sound like a big corporate firm.
- Do not use pushy sales language, scarcity tactics, discounts, or exaggerated guarantees.
- Do not remove or weaken the core offer: free call, free preliminary concept, paid agreement only after alignment.
- Do not overpromise fixed costs or timelines before reviewing project scope.
- Do not write Arabic in stiff classical language or slangy social-media language.
- Do not use generic real-estate copy such as `dream home`, `luxury lifestyle`, `exclusive opportunity`, or `unmatched elegance` unless heavily grounded in architecture.
- Do not frame luxury as size, gold finishes, decoration, or spectacle.
- Do not copy the visual language or imagery of other architecture studios.

Design and visuals:

- Do not use saturated colors, gradients, shadows, heavy animation, parallax, carousels, or autoplay.
- Do not use loud stock imagery, real-estate staging, fake family lifestyle scenes, watermarks, or dramatic sunset cliches.
- Do not compress whitespace just to fit more content.
- Do not letterspace Arabic.
- Do not introduce direction-specific design systems; Arabic and English should feel like the same brand.

Content:

- Do not publish external imagery without a clear commercial-use license and credit record.
- Do not remove concept-study labeling from placeholder projects.
- Do not speak only to aesthetics; connect design to privacy, climate, family life, execution, and long-term value.
- Do not make content too broad. Keep it anchored in private villas, Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, GCC, Obhur, modern Hijazi design, coastal living, and build-ready architectural process.
